# 04-Glossary-of-AI-Terms: Business-Centric Definitions

This glossary translates technical AI jargon into business-oriented language to facilitate clear communication between BAs, engineers, and executives.

## A-G
- **Agentic AI**: AI systems that don't just generate text but can "reason," plan, and use tools to achieve a specific business goal (e.g., resolving a customer refund).
- **Adversarial Attack**: Malicious attempts to manipulate an AI model's behavior by providing deceptive input (e.g., prompt injection).
- **Context Window**: The maximum amount of data (tokens) a model can "remember" or process in a single interaction.
- **Data Moat**: A defensible competitive advantage built on proprietary, high-quality data that competitors cannot easily replicate.
- **EU AI Act**: The world's first comprehensive legal framework for AI, categorizing systems by risk levels (Unacceptable, High, Limited, Minimal).
- **Fine-Tuning**: The process of further training a pre-trained model on a smaller, specialized dataset to improve its performance on a specific task.

## H-P
- **Hallucination**: When an AI model generates false or fabricated information that sounds confident and plausible.
- **Human-in-the-Loop (HITL)**: A design pattern where human intervention or oversight is integrated at critical decision points in an AI-driven process.
- **LLM-as-a-Judge**: Using a high-capability model (e.g., GPT-4) to automatically evaluate and score the performance of other models or agents.
- **Multi-Agent Orchestration**: Coordinating multiple specialized AI agents to work together on a complex, multi-step business process.
- **Outcome-Oriented AI**: Tasking AI with achieving a specific result (e.g., "Reduce shipping delays") rather than executing a discrete task.
- **Prompt Engineering**: The art and science of crafting clear, specific instructions to guide an LLM toward a desired output.

## R-Z
- **RAI (Responsible AI)**: A framework for ensuring AI is developed and deployed ethically, safely, and transparently.
- **RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)**: Grounding an LLM in an organization's private, real-time data by "retrieving" relevant documents before "generating" a response.
- **SLM (Small Language Model)**: A more compact, specialized model that is faster and cheaper to run than a massive LLM, ideal for specific domain tasks.
- **Token Economics**: The cost model for LLM usage, where organizations pay for the volume of data processed (input/output tokens).
- **TCO (Total Cost of Ownership)**: The total cost of building, deploying, and maintaining an AI system, including tokens, infrastructure, and human support.
- **Vector Database**: A specialized database that stores data as "embeddings" (mathematical representations of meaning), enabling fast semantic search.
- **Zero-Shot / Few-Shot**: The ability of a model to perform a task with no examples (zero-shot) or only a few examples (few-shot) provided in the prompt.
