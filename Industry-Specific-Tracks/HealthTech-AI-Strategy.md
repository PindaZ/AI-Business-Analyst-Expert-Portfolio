# AI in HealthTech: Strategic Overlay

The healthcare industry offers massive ROI potential for AI but comes with the highest regulatory and ethical risks. AI-Expert Business Analysts must navigate strict data privacy laws while driving clinical efficiency.

## 🏥 Key Agentic Use Cases
1.  **Ambient Clinical Scribes:** Multi-modal agents that listen to patient consultations, automatically transcribe, and structure the data into the Electronic Health Record (EHR).
2.  **Diagnostic Co-Pilots:** RAG-based systems that cross-reference patient symptoms with millions of medical journals to suggest differential diagnoses to the physician.
3.  **Revenue Cycle Management (RCM) Agents:** Agents that review medical coding against insurance policies to autonomously prevent claim denials.

## 🛡️ Critical Governance & Risk Factors
*   **HIPAA & PHI:** LLMs cannot ingest Protected Health Information (PHI) unless strict Business Associate Agreements (BAAs) with zero-retention policies are in place, or on-premise Small Language Models (SLMs) are utilized.
*   **The "Final Sign-Off":** An AI should never act autonomously in a clinical setting without a Human-in-the-Loop (HITL). The doctor must always legally "sign" the AI's output.
*   **Algorithmic Bias:** If an AI model is trained primarily on data from affluent hospitals, its diagnostic recommendations may be biased against marginalized demographics. The BA must audit the training data origins.

## 📊 Measuring ROI
*   **Reduction in "Pajama Time":** Hours doctors save not typing notes at home.
*   **Claim Denial Rate:** Percentage drop in insurance rejections due to better medical coding.
