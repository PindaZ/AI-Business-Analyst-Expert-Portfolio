# Semantic Search vs. Keyword Search: Business Impact Analysis

In the transition to Generative AI (GenAI) and Agentic AI, the importance of **semantic search vs. keyword search** is becoming increasingly clear. For Agentic AI systems to be truly effective, they must be grounded in an organization's private, real-time context. This necessitates a strategic approach to selecting the right search technology for a given task.

## The Strategic Value of Semantic Search

1.  **Fast Knowledge Retrieval**: Grounding AI agents in an organization's private, real-time context.
2.  **Efficient User Access**: Coordinating multiple specialized models to achieve complex business goals.
3.  **Improved Accuracy and Reliability**: Grounding AI agents in the most relevant data.
4.  **Optimized Performance and Cost**: Selecting the right-sized search technology and optimizing the agent's actions for performance, cost, and latency.

## The Selection Criteria for Business Analysts

### 1. Accuracy and Relevance (Operational Stage)
*   **Focus**: Identifying the volume and diversity of the organization's knowledge assets.
*   **BA Role**: Defining the requirements and constraints for the search technology.

### 2. Integration and Interoperability (Process Stage)
*   **Focus**: Mapping the organization's data sources and tools with the search technology.
*   **BA Role**: Identifying and integrating the organization's data sources and tools.

### 3. Ease of Use and Maintenance (Sustainability Stage)
*   **Focus**: Establishing the skills gap and training plan for the search technology.
*   **BA Role**: Identifying the skills gap and providing the necessary training for AI adoption.

### 4. Cost and Licensing (Financial Stage)
*   **Focus**: Identifying potential for cost savings and cost-effective architectures.
*   **BA Role**: Projecting initial and ongoing costs and expected benefits.

## The Business Analyst's Role in Search Technology Selection

For the BA, selecting the right-sized search technology for a given task involves:

*   **Mapping User Queries to Tasks**: Identifying the intent and complexity of an incoming query.
*   **Defining Success Metrics for Tasks**: Establishing the KPIs and guardrails that govern the model's autonomous actions.
*   **Monitoring and Evaluating Performance**: Tracking the impact of the search technology selection on the organization's overarching business goals.
*   **Identifying Opportunities for Cost Savings**: Identifying potential errors and identifying opportunities for cost savings.

## Conclusion

By strategically selecting the right-sized search technology for a given task, Business Analysts can lead their organizations towards a future of autonomous efficiency and strategic innovation.
