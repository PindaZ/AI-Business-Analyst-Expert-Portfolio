# Knowledge Check: Phase 5 Implementation & Change Management

Test your proficiency in the "Human" side of AI deployments.

---

## Scenario 1: AI Anxiety
**Context:** You are deploying a "Transcription AI" for a legal firm. 
**Problem:** The 10 junior legal assistants who currently do transcription are worried about layoffs. 
**Question:** How should you frame the AI rollout?
- A) "AI is replacing you, but we will find you something else."
- B) "The AI is a 'Co-Pilot' that handles the first draft, allowing you to focus on 'Legal Research and Case Analysis'."
- C) "Don't worry, the AI isn't very good yet."
- D) Say nothing and wait for the results.

**Answer & Rationale:** **B (Co-Pilot)**. 
Frame the AI as an augmentation of the human's higher-level skills. This reduces "AI Anxiety" and encourages the staff to adopt the technology rather than resist it.

---

## Scenario 2: Feedback Loops
**Context:** Your "Customer Support Agent" is consistently giving the wrong answer to a common question about shipping to Alaska. 
**Problem:** The model isn't trained on Alaska's specific logistics. 
**Question:** What is the most effective way for a human manager to fix this *permanently*?
- A) Tell the manager to manually edit every wrong response.
- B) Provide the manager with a "Knowledge Base" UI to upload the correct Alaska shipping policy to the RAG system.
- C) Fine-tune the entire LLM on Alaska's history.
- D) Delete the agent.

**Answer & Rationale:** **B (Knowledge Base)**. 
RAG-based feedback loops allow non-technical SMEs (Subject Matter Experts) to update the agent's knowledge in real-time without retraining the model.

---

## Scenario 3: Adoption Segments
**Context:** You have identified 5 "Innovators" in your department who are secretly using ChatGPT for their work. 
**Question:** What is the most strategic way to use them?
- A) Reprimand them for using unauthorized software.
- B) Make them "AI Champions" who can lead the pilot group and mentor others.
- C) Ignore them.
- D) Ask them to share their passwords.

**Answer & Rationale:** **B (Champions)**. 
Innovators are your best internal advocates. By giving them a formal role, you turn their "Shadow IT" usage into a structured, visible success story for the rest of the team.
