# Lab: The C-Suite Business Case Pitch

## 🎯 Objective
The final step in the AI-BA lifecycle is **Securing Funding**. In this lab, you will draft a compelling "C-Suite Pitch Deck" (Narrative version) for a major AI initiative.

## 🛠️ Scenario Context
**Company:** "Aero-Parts Corp."
**Project:** "Predictive Procurement Agent" - An AI that autonomously orders spare parts 10 days *before* a machine fails.
**The Challenge:** The CFO is skeptical. They think "the current process works fine" and "AI is a buzzword."

---

## 📝 Exercise 1: The "Risk of Inaction" (Opportunity Cost)
If the machine fails, the factory stops for 4 hours, costing the company **$50,000 per hour** in lost production.
*   **Task:** Draft a 1-paragraph "Urgency Statement" that explains the cost of *not* building this AI.

---

## 📊 Exercise 2: Defining the "Value Multiplier"
Beyond cost savings, how does this AI create **Strategic Value**?
1.  **Market Share:** Faster repairs = happier customers.
2.  **Agility:** The AI can react to global supply chain shocks faster than a human buyer.
3.  **Scalability:** The AI can manage 10,000 parts; a human can only manage 200.

**Task:** Choose the strongest "Strategic Value" argument and write the pitch for it.

---

## ✅ Deliverable
A "One-Page Executive Summary" for the CEO/CFO. 
*   **The Problem:** (Cost of downtime).
*   **The Solution:** (Predictive Agent).
*   **The Financials:** (Cost to build vs. Potential savings).
*   **The Strategic Impact:** (Competitive advantage).
*   **The Call to Action:** (Funding requested for the Pilot Phase).
