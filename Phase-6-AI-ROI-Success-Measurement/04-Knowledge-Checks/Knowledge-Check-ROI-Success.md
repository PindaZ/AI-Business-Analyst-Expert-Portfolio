# Knowledge Check: Phase 6 ROI & Success Measurement

Test your ability to prove the financial and strategic value of AI initiatives.

---

## Scenario 1: Calculating ROI
**Context:** Your "Customer Success Agent" costs $100,000/year to host and maintain. 
**Problem:** It saves the company $1.2M in labor costs by autonomously resolving tickets. 
**Question:** What is the ROI (Return on Investment) for this initiative?
- A) 10%
- B) 100%
- C) 1,100% (11x)
- D) 1,200% (12x)

**Answer & Rationale:** **C (11x)**. 
($1.2M Savings - $0.1M Cost) / $0.1M Cost = 11.0. ROI is the *net* benefit divided by the cost.

---

## Scenario 2: Intangible Value
**Context:** Your "AI Strategy Researcher" helps the CEO make a decision in 2 days that normally takes 2 months. 
**Problem:** The CEO's time isn't a direct "line item" on the budget, but the faster decision helped the company beat a competitor to market. 
**Question:** What is this an example of?
- A) Tangible ROI (Hard savings).
- B) Intangible Strategic Value (Agility/Opportunity cost).
- C) Sunk Cost.
- D) Model Overhead.

**Answer & Rationale:** **B (Intangible)**. 
Not all AI value is found in cost reduction. **Decision Velocity** and **Market Agility** are powerful strategic multipliers that an Expert BA must learn to articulate.

---

## Scenario 3: Success Metrics
**Context:** Your "Autonomous Sales Agent" is sending 10,000 emails a day, but only 1% are getting a response.
**Problem:** The "Volume" metric is high, but the "Value" metric is low.
**Question:** As a BA, which metric should you prioritize for the next board meeting?
- A) Total Emails Sent (Input volume).
- B) Conversion Rate per Lead (Outcome quality).
- C) Model Latency (Technical speed).
- D) Token Cost per Email (Efficiency).

**Answer & Rationale:** **B (Conversion Rate)**. 
Board members care about **Outcomes**, not **Activities**. High volume with low conversion is a sign of "AI Spam," not "AI Value."
