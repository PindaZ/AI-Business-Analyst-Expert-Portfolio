# Prompt Injection & Adversarial Attack Risks

In the transition to Generative AI (GenAI) and Agentic AI, the importance of **prompt injection and adversarial attack risks** is becoming increasingly clear. As organizations deploy more specialized AI agents, the need to protect these systems from unauthorized access or malicious manipulation is becoming critical.

## The Challenges of AI Security

AI security is inherently difficult due to the "black-box" nature of some AI models. For Business Analysts (BAs), the main challenges include:

1.  **Complexity and Opacity**: AI systems can be difficult to understand and explain, even for technical experts.
2.  **Autonomous Decision-Making**: Agents can make decisions and take actions in real-time without human intervention.
3.  **Cross-Agent Collaboration**: Multiple specialized agents work together to achieve complex business goals.
4.  **Regulatory Uncertainty**: The legal and regulatory landscape for AI is still evolving.

## The Principles of AI Security

1.  **Protecting User Data and Models**: Ensuring private data stays within the organizational perimeter.
2.  **Implementing Guardrails and Monitoring**: Preventing AI systems from taking harmful or unintended actions.
3.  **Integrating Human Oversight (HITL)**: Designing the AI system to be effectively overseen by natural persons during its operation.
4.  **Incident Reporting and Response**: Establishing a clear process for reporting and responding to AI-related security breaches.

## The Business Analyst's Role in AI Security

For the BA, prompt injection and adversarial attack risks involve:

*   **Mapping AI Roles and Responsibilities**: Identifying the key players, data sources, and dependencies for the AI system.
*   **Defining Success Metrics for Security**: Establishing the KPIs and guardrails that govern the agent's autonomous actions.
*   **Integrating Human Oversight (HITL)**: Determining when a human should review and approve the agent's actions.
*   **Monitoring and Evaluating Performance**: Tracking the impact of the AI system on the business and identifying opportunities for improvement.

## Conclusion

By strategically designing and implementing AI security for prompt injection and adversarial attack risks, Business Analysts can ensure their organization's Agentic AI systems are safe, effective, and consistently aligned with business objectives and ethical standards.
