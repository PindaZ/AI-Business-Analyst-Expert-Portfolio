# Drafting Guardrails for Autonomous Agent Execution

In the second stage of the Generative AI (GenAI) era, the importance of **guardrails for autonomous agent execution** is becoming increasingly clear. For Agentic AI systems to be truly effective and safe, they must operate within defined boundaries and comply with organizational policies.

## The Three Levels of Guardrails

1.  **Policy Guardrails (High-Level)**:
    *   **Focus**: Ensuring the agent's actions are aligned with the organization's overarching values and ethical standards.
    *   **BA Role**: Defining the business logic and ethical constraints for the agent.
2.  **Safety Guardrails (Operational)**:
    *   **Focus**: Preventing the agent from taking harmful or unintended actions.
    *   **BA Role**: Identifying edge cases and determining when human-in-the-loop (HITL) review is required.
3.  **Efficiency Guardrails (Performance)**:
    *   **Focus**: Optimizing the agent's actions for performance, cost, and latency.
    *   **BA Role**: Defining success metrics and risk thresholds for the agent's autonomous actions.

## The Business Analyst's Role in Drafting Guardrails

*   **Risk Assessment and Harm Identification**: Identifying potential risks and harms associated with the agent's autonomous actions.
*   **Defining Acceptable Use and Boundaries**: Clearly articulating the agent's role, responsibilities, and constraints.
*   **Designing Monitoring and Evaluation Processes**: Establishing a system for monitoring the agent's performance and compliance with guardrails.
*   **Integrating Human Oversight (HITL)**: Determining when a human should review and approve the agent's actions.

## Conclusion

By strategically drafting and implementing guardrails, Business Analysts can ensure their organization's Agentic AI systems are safe, effective, and consistently aligned with business objectives and ethical standards.
