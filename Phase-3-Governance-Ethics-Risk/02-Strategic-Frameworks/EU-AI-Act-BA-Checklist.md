# The EU AI Act: A BA's Compliance Checklist

The **EU AI Act** is a landmark regulation that sets a comprehensive legal framework for artificial intelligence (AI) in the European Union. For Business Analysts (BAs) involved in AI initiatives, understanding and complying with this regulation is critical.

## The Four Risk Levels of the EU AI Act

1.  **Unacceptable Risk (Prohibited)**:
    *   **Focus**: AI systems that pose a clear threat to safety, livelihoods, or rights.
    *   **Example**: AI systems for social scoring by governments or real-time remote biometric identification in public spaces.
2.  **High Risk (Regulated)**:
    *   **Focus**: AI systems that have a significant impact on people's safety or fundamental rights.
    *   **Example**: AI systems used in critical infrastructure, education, employment, and essential private and public services.
3.  **Limited Risk (Transparency Obligations)**:
    *   **Focus**: AI systems that have a lower risk but still require transparency.
    *   **Example**: Chatbots, deepfakes, and AI systems for generating or manipulating image, audio, or video content.
4.  **Minimal Risk (No Obligations)**:
    *   **Focus**: AI systems that pose little to no risk.
    *   **Example**: AI-enabled video games or spam filters.

## The Business Analyst's Compliance Checklist

For high-risk AI systems, the BA must ensure compliance with several key requirements:

*   **Data Quality and Governance**: Ensuring training, validation, and testing datasets are relevant, representative, and free of errors.
*   **Transparency and Information to Users**: Providing clear information about the AI system's characteristics, capabilities, and limitations.
*   **Human Oversight**: Designing the AI system to be effectively overseen by natural persons during its operation.
*   **Technical Documentation and Record-Keeping**: Maintaining detailed technical documentation and logs of the AI system's operations.
*   **Accuracy, Robustness, and Cybersecurity**: Ensuring the AI system is accurate, resilient to errors, and protected from unauthorized access or attacks.
*   **Conformity Assessment**: Ensuring the AI system has undergone a conformity assessment before being placed on the market or put into service.

## Conclusion

By understanding and adhering to the EU AI Act, Business Analysts can ensure their organization's AI initiatives are not only compliant with the law but also built on a foundation of safety, transparency, and ethical responsibility.
