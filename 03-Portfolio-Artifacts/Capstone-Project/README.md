# Capstone Project: The Enterprise AI Strategy Dossier

## 🎓 The Ultimate Portfolio Piece

To truly demonstrate your expertise as an AI Business Analyst, you must show the ability to synthesize all 6 phases of the AI lifecycle into a cohesive, executive-ready strategy. 

This Capstone Project is designed to be the crown jewel of your portfolio. Once completed, you can link to it from your resume or LinkedIn profile to prove you can bridge the gap between technical AI engineering and high-level business value.

---

## 🏢 The Scenario: "MedTech Solutions Inc."

You have been hired as the Lead AI Strategist for "MedTech Solutions Inc.", a mid-sized provider of Electronic Health Record (EHR) software for regional hospitals. 

**The Current State:**
*   Hospitals are complaining that doctors spend 2 hours a day manually typing patient notes into MedTech's clunky interface.
*   MedTech's competitors are launching "AI Scribes" that listen to doctor-patient conversations and auto-fill the EHR. MedTech is losing market share.
*   The CEO wants to "add AI" but is terrified of HIPAA violations (patient data privacy) and the cost of building large models.

---

## 📋 Your Deliverable: The Strategy Dossier

Create a comprehensive markdown document (or a linked series of documents) that answers the challenge across all 6 domains. 

### Section 1: Strategic Discovery (Phase 1)
*   Define the core business problem (Outcome-Oriented).
*   Propose the Agentic Solution (e.g., an Ambient Voice Agent + RAG for medical coding).
*   Justify why this is a "Strategic Bet" rather than a "Quick Win."

### Section 2: Data & Enterprise Readiness (Phase 2)
*   Outline the Data Pipeline: How does audio become text, and how does text become structured EHR data?
*   How will MedTech build a "Data Moat" using proprietary medical terminology?

### Section 3: Governance, Ethics & Risk (Phase 3)
*   **Crucial:** Define the HIPAA compliance strategy. (e.g., On-premise SLMs vs. Zero-Retention API agreements).
*   Design the HITL (Human-in-the-Loop) guardrail: *The AI cannot finalize a diagnosis; the doctor must review and sign.*

### Section 4: Conceptual Architecture (Phase 4)
*   Include a `Mermaid.js` diagram showing the flow: Patient Room (Audio) -> Whisper API (Transcription) -> PII Scrubber -> LLM (Summarization) -> EHR Database.

### Section 5: Change Management (Phase 5)
*   How will you train the doctors to trust the system? 
*   How will you handle the "AI Anxiety" of the medical coders who fear job replacement?

### Section 6: ROI & Business Case (Phase 6)
*   Estimate the TCO (Total Cost of Ownership) per doctor, per month (compute costs).
*   Estimate the Value generated (2 hours saved per day = X more patients seen = $Y revenue increase).
*   Present the final ROI multiplier.
