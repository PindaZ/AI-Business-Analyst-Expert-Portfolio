# Lab: Identifying and Prioritizing Agentic Use Cases

## 🎯 Objective
Transition from theoretical knowledge of AI Strategy to practical application. In this lab, you will act as the Lead AI Business Analyst for a fictitious enterprise, "Global Logistics Corp" (GLC). You will evaluate a list of raw business pain points and filter them into high-value AI use cases using an Opportunity Matrix.

## 🏢 Scenario Context
**Company:** Global Logistics Corp (GLC)
**Current State:** GLC is struggling with rising operational costs, customer dissatisfaction due to delayed shipments, and an overwhelmed IT support desk.
**Your Mandate:** The CIO has asked you to identify the top 3 AI initiatives they should fund this year.

## 🛠️ The Raw Data: Stakeholder Pain Points
Here is the unstructured feedback gathered from recent stakeholder interviews:

1.  *"Our customer service reps spend 40% of their day answering 'Where is my order?' emails by looking up tracking numbers in three different systems."* (Customer Success)
2.  *"We have a massive database of past shipping delays and weather patterns, but route planners still mostly guess when scheduling."* (Operations)
3.  *"HR is drowning in emails asking about benefits policies and vacation days. The intranet search is terrible."* (HR)
4.  *"We need a better logo for our new premium shipping tier."* (Marketing)
5.  *"When a supplier delays a shipment of raw materials, it takes us 3 days to figure out which downstream customer orders will be impacted."* (Supply Chain)

## 📝 Exercise 1: Use Case Formulation
Convert the raw pain points above into specific **Agentic AI Use Cases**. 
*Remember the shift to Outcome-Oriented AI. Don't just build a chatbot; build an agent that solves the problem.*

**Template:**
*   **Pain Point #:**
*   **Proposed AI Solution:** (e.g., "A multi-modal RAG agent that...")
*   **Desired Business Outcome:** (e.g., "Reduce average handle time by...")

## 📊 Exercise 2: The Prioritization Matrix
Plot your formulated use cases on a 2x2 matrix based on **Business Value** (High/Low) and **Implementation Complexity** (High/Low).

*   **High Value / Low Complexity (Quick Wins):** Fund these immediately.
*   **High Value / High Complexity (Strategic Bets):** Long-term transformative projects.
*   **Low Value / Low Complexity (Fill-ins):** Do these if resources allow.
*   **Low Value / High Complexity (Money Pits):** Avoid.

*Questions to consider when assessing complexity:*
*   Do we have the data? Is it clean?
*   Does this require complex system integrations?
*   Are there severe regulatory or ethical risks?

## ✅ Deliverable
Draft a 1-page Executive Summary for the CIO outlining your top 3 recommended use cases, justifying your choices based on your Prioritization Matrix.
