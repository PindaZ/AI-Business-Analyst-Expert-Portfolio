# Economics of Generative AI: Token-Based Value Models

Understanding the unit economics of Generative AI is critical for any Business Analyst transitioning into the AI space. Unlike traditional software, where marginal costs are near zero, GenAI incurs a specific cost for every interaction, measured in **tokens**.

## 1. The Token Economy
Tokens are the fundamental units of text processing for LLMs. A rule of thumb is that 1,000 tokens equal approximately 750 words.
- **Input Tokens:** The cost of the prompt sent to the model (context).
- **Output Tokens:** The cost of the response generated by the model.

**Strategic Insight:** As a BA, you must optimize "Context Window" management. Passing too much irrelevant data into a prompt doesn't just increase latency; it directly erodes the project's profit margin.

## 2. Cost Structures: Build vs. Buy vs. Tune
| Model Approach | Cost Driver | Business Implication |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Commodity APIs** (e.g., GPT-4o) | Pay-per-token | Low upfront cost, but variable and potentially high at scale. |
| **Open-Source / Self-Hosted** (e.g., Llama 3) | Infrastructure (GPU/VRAM) | High fixed costs, but marginal cost per token becomes negligible at high volumes. |
| **Fine-Tuning** | Compute + Specialized Talent | High upfront R&D, but creates a specific "Data Moat" and efficiency for narrow tasks. |

## 3. The "Intelligence-per-Dollar" Ratio
The goal of the AI-BA is to maximize the "Intelligence-per-Dollar." 
- For simple routing or summarization, use a **Small Language Model (SLM)** like Phi-3 or GPT-4o-mini.
- For complex strategic reasoning or multi-step orchestration, reserve budget for **frontier models**.

## 4. Total Cost of Ownership (TCO) Factors
Beyond tokens, the BA must factor in:
1. **Data Pipeline Maintenance:** Keeping RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) databases fresh.
2. **Human-in-the-Loop (HITL) Costs:** The cost of expert review for high-risk outputs.
3. **Observability & Evaluation:** The compute cost of running "LLM-as-a-Judge" to verify accuracy.

## Conclusion
A successful AI initiative requires a BA who treats tokens like a finite resource. By balancing model tiering and context optimization, you ensure that the AI solution delivers a positive financial return.
