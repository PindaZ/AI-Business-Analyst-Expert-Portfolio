# Knowledge Check: AI Prioritization Scenarios

Test your ability to apply the **AI Opportunity Matrix** and **Value Chain Analysis** to real-world business challenges.

---

## Scenario 1: The Overwhelmed HR Department
**Context:** A global firm with 50,000 employees has an HR team that receives 2,000 emails a day asking about the "Work from Home Policy" and "Vacation Accrual Rules." 
**Question:** Where does this fit on the AI Opportunity Matrix?
- A) High Value / High Complexity (Strategic Bet)
- B) High Value / Low Complexity (Quick Win)
- C) Low Value / Low Complexity (Fill-in)
- D) Low Value / High Complexity (Money Pit)

**Answer & Rationale:** **B (Quick Win)**. 
The knowledge is static (Company Policy), the task is repetitive, and the risk of a "wrong answer" is low-to-moderate. A RAG-based internal agent can handle 90% of these queries with high accuracy.

---

## Scenario 2: The Precision Manufacturing Moat
**Context:** A boutique aerospace manufacturer has 40 years of proprietary alloy testing data that no one else in the world possesses. They want to build an AI that suggests new alloy compositions.
**Question:** From a Value Chain perspective, what is the primary strategic benefit?
- A) Cost Reduction in Operations
- B) Creating a Strategic Moat through Proprietary Data
- C) Enhancing Customer Service
- D) Improving Inbound Logistics

**Answer & Rationale:** **B (Strategic Moat)**. 
Because the data is unique and inaccessible to competitors (including frontier model labs like OpenAI), the resulting AI will provide a capability that cannot be commoditized. This is a "Strategic Bet" with massive long-term value.

---

## Scenario 3: The "Shiny Object" Trap
**Context:** The CEO wants to use a Generative AI image generator to create "cool, futuristic headshots" for the executive leadership team's "About Us" page.
**Question:** How should a Senior AI-BA categorize this request?
- A) Top Priority (High Visibility)
- B) Quick Win (Easy implementation)
- C) Low Value / Low Complexity (Fill-in / Distraction)
- D) Strategic Bet (Core Innovation)

**Answer & Rationale:** **C (Fill-in / Distraction)**. 
While easy to do, it has near-zero impact on the organization's core value chain or ROI. A Senior BA should manage expectations and ensure this doesn't divert resources from high-impact projects like supply chain optimization.
