# Lab: Drafting an Enterprise AI Policy

## 🎯 Objective
In this lab, you will draft the "Version 1.0" of an Enterprise AI Policy for a mid-sized financial services firm, "SecureBank."

## 🛠️ Scenario Context
**The Challenge:** SecureBank's employees are already using ChatGPT for everything from writing emails to summarizing confidential board meeting transcripts. There is currently NO formal policy.
**Your Goal:** Create a policy that balances **Innovation** with **Security**.

---

## 📝 Exercise 1: The "Acceptable Use" Tiers
Categorize the following tasks into a "Traffic Light" system:
- **🟢 Green (Self-Service):** e.g., Drafting marketing emails (No customer data).
- **🟡 Yellow (Manager Approval Needed):** e.g., Summarizing meeting transcripts with internal-only data.
- **🔴 Red (Prohibited):** e.g., Uploading raw customer social security numbers or credit card info to a public LLM.

---

## 📊 Exercise 2: Defining "Ownership & Accountability"
If an AI agent accidentally leaks a customer's balance, who is responsible?
*   The Developer who wrote the code?
*   The BA who defined the requirements?
*   The End-User who triggered the agent?
*   The Model Provider (e.g., OpenAI/Anthropic)?

**Task:** Write a "Responsibility Clause" (1 paragraph) for the policy that clarifies this for employees.

---

## ✅ Deliverable
A 1-page "AI Policy Executive Summary." 
*   **Section 1:** Purpose of the policy.
*   **Section 2:** Data Privacy Guardrails.
*   **Section 3:** Mandatory "Human Review" requirements.
*   **Section 4:** Consequences of non-compliance.
